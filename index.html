Fun Facts about penguins!

    Fun Facts
  • Penguins are carnivorous animals and catch their prey live in the sea.
  • Penguins vary widely in shape and size, with the emperor penguin being the largest weighing up to 41 kilograms and the little penguin being the smallest, averaging only 1 kilogram.
  • Chinstrap penguins on Zavodovski Island in the South Sandwich Islands currently hold the Guinness World Record for largest penguin colony, consisting of approximately 2 million penguins.
  • Penguins do not have teeth but do have spines on both their tongues and the inside of their beaks to help them grip their prey.
  • Scientists have discovered some penguins swallowing small stones and pebbles. Scientists speculate that this could aid the digestion of food or reduce buoyancy when diving.
  • Penguins cannot breathe underwater; the duration of their dives varies between species, from 7 to 20 minutes.
  • Like dogs, penguins pant to release heat and stay cool.
  • Penguins can drink salt water as their supraorbital gland filters the salt from their bloodstream.
  • Penguins have large solid bones without air spaces to facilitate diving by reducing buoyancy.
  • Emperor penguins have the highest density of feathers in the world.
